Light and Geometric Optics

How does Light Travel

Light travels through a medium in straight lines. A medium is any substance through which energy can be transferred. There is also radiation which is a method of energy transfer without the need of a medium. An electromagnetic wave is a wave which has both electric and magnetic properties. It does not require a medium either. Light is produced from all things you see. Luminous means that it produces its own light and non-luminous means it doesn’t produce light. Light bulbs are a source of artificial light and there are many types of them. Incandescent light bulbs produce light as a result of high temperatures. Light made by a process called electric discharge is produced by passing an electric current through a gas. A fluorescent light bulb(shown below) makes light by an electric discharge exciting the mercury atoms which then release UV light and that turns into visible light when it hits the side of the tube. A LED(Light Emitting Diode) is the light produced as a result of an electric current flowing through semiconductors

Mirrors

Mirrors use the property of reflection which is a change in the direction of a light ray, to create an image. A few terms to remember are: normal which is a line perpendicular to a surface, incident ray which is the ray of light travelling towards a surface, reflected ray which is the ray formed when the incident ray meets the normal, the angle of incidence is the angle between the normal and the incident ray, the angle of reflection is the angle between the normal and the reflected ray. According to the law of reflection it says: the normal, incident ray and reflected ray should be in the same plane and the angle of incidence must equal the angle of reflection.

Different Types of Mirrors

There are many types of mirrors as you may have seen in a fun house. There is the plane mirror which is a regular flat mirror, there is a concave mirror which has the reflective surface curved inwards, and there is the convex mirrors which have the reflective surface curved outwards. Images are formed when light rays meet and there are two types of images. There is the virtual image in which it seems as if the light rays are meeting but they actually aren’t. There is a real image where the light rays are actually meeting. All images formed on a plane mirror, convex mirror and beyond some point on a concave mirror will be virtual. Images formed on a concave mirror when the object is more than a certain distance(focal point) will be real. Also, all real images will be inverted. There are also a few formulas used to calculate the distance and height of the object, its image, the magnification and the focal length which is half of the distance between the mirror and the center of the mirror(Center of Curvature). The mirror equation is 1/f = 1/di + 1/do where f is the focal length, di is the distance to the image and do is the distance to the object. The second equation known as the magnification equation is m = hi/ho = -di/do where m is the magnification, hi is the height of the image and ho is the height of the object.
